# Benchmark: short story

Two drafts of the same constraint. Use these to verify that a voice profile produces distinct output — if the generated draft reads like the AI-slop example rather than the in-voice example, the profile isn't working.

## The constraint

Write 100-200 words of flash fiction. Single scene. No dialog. Subject: **the first coffee of the morning on a day you're dreading.**

## In-voice draft (good)

The Moka pot hissed once and I killed the burner a half-second late. I'd ground the beans coarser than usual — I didn't want the bitterness competing for attention today, not with the 10 a.m. doing all that work for free. Yesterday's toast crumbs were on the counter. I left them. Outside, the neighbor's kid was loading a bike into the back of a Subaru, sideways, and for half a second I wanted to be him: nine years old, not the one doing the explaining. I poured into the blue cup — the one Sara gave me before she moved. The first sip was hot enough to remind me I still had a tongue. I drank the second standing at the window. The bike went in. The dad knew what he was doing. I set the cup down, opened the laptop, and watched the Zoom icon bounce.

## AI-slop draft (bad)

The coffee brewed itself slowly, as if it knew the day needed to be held back — every drop a small rebellion against what was coming. Steam rose in spirals, weightless yet heavy with all the mornings that had come before, and the kitchen was quiet in that particular way that kitchens get quiet when you're alone with something you don't want to face. It wasn't just coffee. It was permission. Permission to sit for a moment longer, permission to pretend the hours ahead hadn't already begun. Somewhere in the back of her mind, she could hear the clock doing its work, quietly, relentlessly, marking the time she'd borrowed. She brought the mug to her lips, and in that simple gesture, there was everything: the fear, the resolve, the small brave act of beginning. She took a sip. The day, like it always did, was waiting.

## What to look for

The in-voice draft has specific nouns (Moka pot, 10 a.m. meeting, Subaru, blue cup, Sara). The AI-slop draft has abstract ones (steam, mornings, permission). The in-voice draft lets one thing happen — a cup gets set down, a Zoom icon bounces. The AI-slop draft narrates feelings about things that didn't happen ("the small brave act of beginning"). A voice profile that generates output resembling the second draft is a profile not doing its job.
