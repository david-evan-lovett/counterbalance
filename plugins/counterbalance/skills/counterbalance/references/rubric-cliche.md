# Cliche Hunter Rubric

Cliches are phrases so overused they've lost specificity. AI writing systems generate them at scale. These sections flag the most common tells: generic AI phrases, stale business metaphors, salesy cadence patterns, and metaphors stretched across multiple paragraphs.

Business and tech clichés that drain specificity:

## Oily cadence patterns

Sentences structured for salesiness rather than clarity. Watch for:

- Tricolon followed by punch line: `"Done it. Loved it. Shipped it."` Three short beats then a flip.
- Rhetorical question sandwich: open with "What if...?", then answer it, then pose it as something settled.
- Triple-adjective stacking before the noun: `"elegant, powerful, revolutionary solution"` — the adjectives outnumber the substance.
- Repeated short sentences in a row: fragment, fragment, fragment. Feels breathless and synthetic.

## Extended metaphors

Metaphors sustained across multiple paragraphs. Flags include:

- A metaphor introduced in one paragraph that continues into the next: "laying the foundation" in paragraph 1, then "building walls" in paragraph 2, then "painting windows" in paragraph 3.
- Architecture metaphors stretched across a whole section (foundation, structure, pillars, roof).
- Journey metaphors: "set out," "along the way," "destination."
- Gardening metaphors: "plant," "grow," "harvest," "weed."

Any metaphor that would need explanation if split across pages is too extended.
