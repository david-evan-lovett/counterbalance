# Benchmark: poem

Two drafts of the same constraint. The pair exists so you can sanity-check a voice profile: if the generated poem reads like the AI-slop example, the profile isn't earning its keep.

## The constraint

Write 8-16 lines of free verse. Concrete imagery. Subject: **the moment you realize something you've been avoiding is actually load-bearing.**

## In-voice draft (good)

The rotted fence post held the gate.
I'd marked it for replacement in March,
then again in May,
then I stopped writing it down.
This morning the snow pulled the whole line sideways.
The post didn't snap.
The two I'd replaced last spring did.
I stood in the yard with a shovel
and the dog I'd been meaning to take to the vet,
and I thought about the roof,
and the lease I hadn't renewed,
and the email I'd been drafting since Tuesday.
The dog sat on my boot.
The fence stayed up.
I went inside and drank water from the tap.

## AI-slop draft (bad)

There is a weight to absence,
a quiet gravity in the spaces between
the things we choose to see
and the things we step around.
I had walked past it for years,
that small unremarkable presence,
thinking it would fold into the background
the way unfinished things always do.
But in the silence of that morning,
as the light came slanting through the kitchen window,
I understood:
what I had been avoiding
was the very thing
holding me up.

## What to look for

The in-voice draft names things — rotted fence post, March, the dog, the shovel, the tap. The AI-slop draft names feelings about things — "weight of absence," "quiet gravity," "unremarkable presence." The in-voice draft lets a moment happen without naming it; the AI-slop draft explains itself in the final three lines. A voice profile that produces the second shape is not a voice profile, it's a mood board.
